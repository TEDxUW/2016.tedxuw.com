FAQ for Webcast Partners to 2016 TEDxUW

Why become a webcast partner to 2016 TEDxUW live event?

  • TEDxUW is a great opportunity to organize an event and associate with the University of Waterloo.
  • TEDxUW is a simple way to engage your community in a conversation about the latest developments in science and technology.
  • TEDxUW webcast partners will be featured in a dedicated section on the event website and we will provide a dedicated web address with high-quality streaming and relevant technical support.

What is involved in becoming a webcast partner?

5 Simple Steps

  • Book a room where you can hold the webcast event (with webcast equipment, internet connection, screen and chairs);
  • Invite people in your community with whom your institution could engage in a constructive dialogue;
  • Promote the event within your community using TEDxUW posters and press release templates provided by our team;
  • Organize a reception during or after the event (the conversations after the talks are among the most memorable parts of a TEDx event!);
  • Send us photos/videos of your event.

What will the TEDxUW team help with?

TEDxUW will provide everything you need to make sure your webcast partner event is a success:

  • A running list of webcast partners on the TEDxUW homepage and social pages;
  • A poster template will be made available for you to download and print;
  • A press release will be made available for customization and distribution in your own community;
  • An HD webcast feed will be made available and direct simulcast event audience participation will be made possible through TEDxUW social media.
